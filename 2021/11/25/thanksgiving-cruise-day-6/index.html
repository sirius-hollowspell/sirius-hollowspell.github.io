Thanksgiving Cruise Day 6

Sirius Hollowspell
2021-11-25
curacao, windjammer

After waking up, we looked out our balcony to look out at Curaçao, and I got some beautiful pictures of Curaçao. We went to our usual breakfast, the Windjammer! When we finished eating, we got off the boat and found our tour guide. The first thing we saw in Curaçao was a shallow lake with lots of flamingos, and it was so cool! I got some pretty cool pictures of the flamingos. On the way to see the turtles, we stopped by a shop where Daddy and the tour guide ate and drank from a coconut. Mama did try the liquid in the coconut, but she didn’t like it. Once the tour guide and daddy finished eating their coconuts, we got back into the tour guide’s car and headed to the beach with the turtles. There were many people at the beach, snorkeling to see the turtles. I managed to get some cool pictures of the turtles while we were there. When I finished taking pictures, we went to one of the most popular beaches on Curaçao. Daddy got out of the car and checked it out while Mama and I stayed in the car. After Daddy got some pictures and was done, we left the beach and headed over to the distillery where we saw the process of how the make the Curaçao drink. While we were at the distillery, Daddy bought some Curaçao from the shop. We had finished with everything we wanted to do for the day, so our guide drove us back to the ship, and we boarded. We hadn’t eaten lunch in Curaçao, so we ate at the Windjammer for lunch again, and because this day was Thanksgiving, in the cheeses, there were little Happy Thanksgiving flags in them. We made some thanksgiving phone calls after we finished eating lunch. We went to the Skypad. The Skypad is an activity where you can where a VR headset and bungee jump. I decided to wear the headset and jump while Mama videoed and took pictures of me. The experience you get when wearing the VR is sensational! It’s thrilling to be jumping and “playing” a game at the same time. Mama and I then watched the sunset, where I got more beautiful pictures. Our Thanksgiving dinner was at Giovanni’s Italian Kitchen, and it was very delicious as always. When we got back to the room, Eddie, our housekeeper, had made us a turkey towel animal, and it was SUPER CUTE! Mama and Daddy had dessert at Giovanni’s, but I didn’t have dessert there, so I ordered some chocolate chip cookies from room service.
